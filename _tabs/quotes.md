This is a small collection of quotes I keep coming back to. The useful ones are not motivational wallpaper. They are reminders about engineering, systems, learning, and how easy it is to fool yourself.

## Engineering

> Simplicity is prerequisite for reliability.
>
> -- Edsger W. Dijkstra

> Programs must be written for people to read, and only incidentally for machines to execute.
>
> -- Harold Abelson and Gerald Jay Sussman

> Make it work, make it right, make it fast.
>
> -- Kent Beck

## Systems

> All models are wrong, but some are useful.
>
> -- George Box

> Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler.
>
> -- Commonly attributed to Albert Einstein

> The purpose of abstraction is not to be vague, but to create a new semantic level.
>
> -- Edsger W. Dijkstra

## Learning

> If you cannot explain something in simple terms, you do not understand it.
>
> -- Richard Feynman

> Plans are worthless, but planning is everything.
>
> -- Dwight D. Eisenhower

> The best way to predict the future is to invent it.
>
> -- Alan Kay

## Life

> Your time is limited, so do not waste it living someone else's life.
>
> -- Steve Jobs

> We are what we repeatedly do.
>
> -- Will Durant, summarizing Aristotle

> Waste no more time arguing what a good man should be. Be one.
>
> -- Marcus Aurelius
